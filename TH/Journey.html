Climbing The Code Ladder

My Journey, So far...

My code journey began when I started doing a college course called ECDL which stands for European Computer Driving License. ECDL does teach a little bit of code but nothing more than very very basic level knowledge, this is what grabbed my interest in code.

I completed my ECDL and then went onto a 2 year course at Stockport college doing general computing which involved Visual Basic programming language. I successfully completed that course and discovered that I could enter into university. I looked at my local area and discovered at least 2 universities, one of them being Salford. I checked Salford out and saw that they did a Software Engineering degree, so I applied. Sadly, due to the banking crisis, I had no other choice but to decline the university's offer of a position in their Software Engineering degree.

I looked around for alternative places to study Software and found The Open University, which is a company that does courses that you can do from your place of residence or anywhere with an internet connection. Sadly, after many attempts, I didn't pass. It was at this point that I was beginning to get very worried for my future and my prospects. Thankfully, after a bit of online searching, I found Treehouse! Upon finding Treehouse, I began to check what they offered. I saw that they offered code courses but not in Visual Basic. I found that they tought HTML, CSS and JavaScript instead, so I had almost no choice but to switch languages. I tried a few of their courses and discovered that I liked them. I also saw that they offered degrees called "Techdegrees" so I enrolled.

Since my enrollment onto the Techdegree, I have slowly made progress in learning HTML, CSS & JavaScript and now I'm not far from finishing the degree.

My Intentions

I intend to work for someone/a company as a Front-End Web Developer. I intend to learn from others whilst gaining experience to make myself better. Earn promotions and climb the so-called ladder. Maybe perhaps one day I'll own a company?
